## 1. Introduction
The **Digital India** programme was launched by Prime Minister Narendra Modi on **July 1, 2015**, at the Indira Gandhi Indoor Stadium in Delhi. The event saw participation from major industrial leaders like Mukesh Ambani (RIL), Cyrus Mistry (Tata Group), and Azim Premji (Wipro), who gathered to discuss taking the digital revolution to the masses.

## 2. What is Digital India?
It is a flagship initiative by the government to transform India into a **digitally empowered knowledge economy**.
* **The Goal:** To ensure that government services are available to citizens electronically and to leverage the latest information and communication technology.
* **Budget & Schemes:** It includes various schemes worth over **Rs 1 lakh crore**. Digital Locker, e-eduction, e-health, e-sign and national scholarship portal.
* **Nodal Agency:** The Ministry of Communications and IT is responsible for implementing the programme.

### Key Infrastructure & Tools:
* **Digital Locker:** A platform for issuance and verification of documents & certificates digitally, eliminating the use of physical documents.
* **e-Education & e-Health:** Digital platforms to provide educational resources and healthcare services remotely.
* **e-Sign:** An online service that allows citizens to digitally sign a document.
* **BharatNet:** A project aimed at providing high-speed digital connectivity to rural areas (initially in 11 states).
* **Next Generation Network (NGN):** Advanced networking technology that carries all information and services (voice, data, and media) by encapsulating them into packets.

## 3. Digital India Apps
The government launched several mobile applications to provide services on the go:
* **Digital India Portal:** The central hub for all digital services.
* **MyGov Mobile App:** A platform for citizen engagement and crowdsourcing ideas for governance.
* **Swachh Bharat Mission App:** To support the national cleanliness campaign.
* **Aadhaar Mobile Update App:** To manage and update Aadhaar details via mobile.

## 4. The Three Visions of Digital India
1.  **Digital Infrastructure as a Utility to Every Citizen:** Providing high-speed internet as a core utility, similar to water or electricity, and ensuring a safe digital identity (Aadhaar).
2.  **Governance & Services on Demand:** Seamlessly integrating services across departments and making them available in real-time on mobile and online platforms.
3.  **Digital Empowerment of Citizens:** Providing digital literacy and making digital resources available in Indian languages so everyone can participate.

## 5. The Nine Pillars of Digital India
These represent the specific areas the government is focusing on to achieve the digital revolution:
1.  **Broadband Highways:** Building the infrastructure for high-speed internet across the country.
2.  **Universal Access to Phones:** Ensuring mobile connectivity reaches every corner of India.
3.  **Public Internet Access Programme:** Setting up common service centers and post offices as internet hubs.
4.  **e-Governance:** Reforming government processes through technology to make them more efficient.
5.  **e-Kranti:** Electronic delivery of services like education, health, and justice.
6.  **Information for All:** Open access to data and documents for citizens.
7.  **Electronics Manufacturing:** Targeting **NET ZERO Imports** by encouraging local manufacturing of electronic goods.
8.  **IT for Jobs:** Training people in smaller towns and villages for IT-related employment.
9.  **Early Harvest Programmes:** Projects that could be implemented quickly, such as Wi-Fi in all universities and public Wi-Fi hotspots.

## 6. Targeted Impact (Projected for 2019-2020)
The programme aimed for massive scale in its reach and job creation:
* **Connectivity:** Broadband in 2.5 lakh villages and universal phone connectivity.
* **Access:** 400,000 Public Internet Access Points and Wi-Fi in 2.5 lakh schools and all universities.
* **Economic Goals:** Net Zero Imports in electronics by 2020.
* **Job Creation:** Aimed to create **1.7 crore direct jobs** and at least **8.5 crore indirect jobs**.
* **Training:** 1.7 crore people trained for IT, Telecom, and Electronics sectors.
* **Leadership:** Positioning India as a global leader in IT use for services like health, education, and banking.
