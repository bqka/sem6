## 1. Introduction to E-Governance
E-Governance represents the role of Information and Communication Technology (ICT) in public administration. While governments have always managed information, the advent of the World Wide Web in the nineties provided a clearer avenue for Government-to-Citizen (G-to-C) interactions. Governments began adopting IT to enhance efficiency, transparency, accountability, and citizen orientation.

**Explanation:** It’s basically moving government paperwork and services onto the internet so things work faster and more openly for regular people.

## 2. The Relationship: Government, Governance, and Democracy
These three concepts represent different perspectives of political entities:
* **Government (Institutional View):** The formal apparatus whose foremost job is to focus society on achieving the public interest.
* **Democracy (Legitimacy View):** The view focused on the legitimacy of the system.
* **Governance (Regulatory View):** Describes the links between government and its broader political, social, and administrative environment; it is the outcome experienced by the governed.

## 3. Key Connotations & Definitions
The discipline of E-Governance is divided into several identifiable categories:
* **E-Administration:** Using ICTs to modernize the state, including computerization of records and MIS data repositories.
* **E-Services:** Bringing the state closer to citizens via online services. Together, E-administration and E-services constitute **E-Government**.
* **E-Governance:** A broader concept that improves the government's ability to address society's needs, covering strategic planning and development goals.
* **E-Democracy:** Using IT to facilitate participation from all sections of society, emphasizing transparency and accountability (e.g., online grievance forums, e-referendums).

**Explanation:** E-Government is about "providing services" (like a shop), while E-Governance is about "shaping society" and letting people participate in decisions.

## 5. Strategic Objectives
* **Internal:** To facilitate speedy, transparent, accountable, and efficient processes for performing government administration (back-office), leading to significant cost savings.
* **External:** To fulfill the public’s needs by simplifying interactions with various online services (front-office).

## 6. E-Governance in India
### Origins
Originated in the 1970s with focus on defense, economic monitoring, and census. A major milestone (watershed) was the **National Informatics Center (NIC)** connecting all district headquarters in the 1980s. 

### National E-Governance Plan (NeGP)
Established to lay the foundation for long-term growth, focusing on core infrastructure, policies, and "Mission Mode Projects" to create citizen-centric governance.

## 7. Implementation Measures & Workplan
Before opting for E-Governance, governments must consider:
* **E-Readiness:** Assessment of human resources, budgets, and society's readiness.
* **Infrastructure:** Investment in telecommunications and high internet connectivity speed.
* **Legal Framework:** Establishing cyber laws to support policy objectives.
* **Content & Training:** Developing local language interfaces and competency building for personnel.
* **Open Source Strategic Choice:** Many governments (like MP and Maharashtra) prefer Open Source (Linux) over proprietary software (Windows) to reduce costs, ensure security of public knowledge, and support local software developers.
